What is Powerlifting?

Powerlifting is a strength sport with competitions on three lifts: squat, bench press, and deadlift. For each lift, the athlete has three attempts to lift maximal weight for a single repetition. Each competition is divided based on gender, age, and different weight classes. For example, the USA Powerlifting (USAPL) federation has eight weight classes in 2019: 43 kg, 47 kg, 52 kg, 57 kg, 63 kg, 72 kg, 84 kg, 84+ kg. Powerlifting federations usally change their weight classes every few years. Also, competitions can be equipped or raw. Equipped competitions allow the lifter to use certain equipment that can help boost up the total amount of weight that they can lift, such as a supportive suit, a bench shirt, and knee sleeves. In contrast, the raw competitions do not permit these types of equipment.

Recommended Resources

  • Starting Strength
    • The principles of Starting Strength form a practical, easy, and logical approach to strength training. The book explains the most effective way to train with barbells, which is critical to improve your strength, your life, and your health. It details the proper techniques for the squat, bench press, and deadlift.
  • Practical Programming for Strength Training
    • Practical Programming for Strength Training discusses the topic of training. It explains the fundamentals of the process, from the principal anatomy of adaptation to the particular programs that apply these methods to beginning, intermediate, and advanced lifters.
  • The Cube Method
    • The Cube Method is a powerlifting training program in which lifters have taken their strength to the next level. It integrates and alternates between Heavy Days, Rep Days, and Explosive Days in an unprecedented manner. The book consists of the journey and method behind the Cube, gym training, assistance work, warm ups, and conditioning.
